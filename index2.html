MY Life

Autobiography

I, Albert Einstein, born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, embarked on a journey fueled by boundless curiosity. My early years were marked by relentless questioning and unconventional thinking. I ventured to the Polytechnic Institute in Zurich, Switzerland, where I met my life partner, Mileva Maric, and embarked on a path that led to groundbreaking discoveries. In 1905, I introduced the theory of special relativity, unveiling the concept that time and space are intertwined, giving rise to my famous equation, E=mc². In 1915, I revealed my theory of general relativity, transforming our understanding of gravity. Beyond science, I championed peace and social justice, advocating for disarmament and pacifism. In 1921, I received the Nobel Prize in Physics for my work on the photoelectric effect.

About

One cannot discuss my work without mentioning my most famous equation, E=mc². This equation, which states that energy equals mass times the speed of light squared, fundamentally altered the foundations of physics. It revealed the profound connection between energy and mass, igniting a scientific revolution.
